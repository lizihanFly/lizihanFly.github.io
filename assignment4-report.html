1. Project Summary

Defend Your Thesis is an original room-based, top-down roguelite shooter built with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and the Canvas 2D API. The player clears academic combat rooms, opens evidence chests, collects weapons of five qualities, and defeats a boss committee at the end of each floor.

The structure is inspired by broad room-based action-roguelite conventions, including games such as Soul Knight. All code, interface text, graphics, enemies, weapon names, balance values, and academic theming are original for this assignment.

3. Gameplay

Every floor contains four standard research rooms and a boss chamber. The player defeats all enemies, opens an evidence chest, evaluates the dropped weapon, and enters the next portal. Ten standard layouts, three boss layouts, and four visual themes use modeled bookshelves, desks, server racks, laboratory vats, and pillars instead of identical blocks.

Weapon qualities and inventory

The two-slot inventory supports fifteen weapons with distinct Canvas silhouettes. Behaviors include spread fire, piercing, explosions, homing, chain lightning, expanding flame, and ricochet projectiles. Each weapon has its own magazine, reserve ammunition, reload time, firing delay, spread, and projectile speed. Press F to pick up, Q to swap, and R to reload.

Right click or X performs a close-range melee strike that can damage enemies and remove hostile projectiles. Firing an empty weapon automatically uses this strike while reloading.

Weapon visual language

Five original technology houses give the arsenal consistent visual families: Axiom mechanical receivers, Vector compact fins, Forge industrial barrels, Helix energy rails, and Relic geometric technology. The design applies general lessons from mainstream loot shooters without copying a commercial weapon or asset.

Weapons now use animated energy cores, rarity-dependent rails and orbiting particles, actual recoil, muzzle flash, barrel heat, ejected casings, model-specific projectiles, impact sparks, shock rings, and holographic loot displays.

Characters, enemies, and talents

The Analyst has stronger weapon damage and can freeze threats. The Hacker moves faster and releases an EMP pulse. The Writer has more integrity and fires a radial Citation Storm. All characters can dash.

Eight enemy types include fast pursuit, long-range sniping, ranged fire, splitting, armor, projectile volleys, and boss summons. After defeating the Final Committee, the player chooses one permanent talent before entering a harder floor.

Data objects define weapon bases, five quality multipliers, characters, enemies, and talents separately from runtime state. The requestAnimationFrame loop uses capped delta time for refresh-rate-independent movement.

5. AI-Assisted Architecture

An AI coding agent inspected the existing static repository and helped turn the assignment brief into a framework-free Canvas application compatible with GitHub Pages. It helped design the data-driven loot model, state transitions, collision system, shared desktop/mobile actions, documentation, and browser tests.

A framework was intentionally avoided because the project has one Canvas scene, no server state, and no need for a build system.

6. AI-Assisted Problem Solving

Weapon generation

A weapon is generated by combining one base definition with one quality multiplier. This avoids duplicating every weapon five times and keeps balancing centralized.

Inventory replacement

New weapons fill an empty slot first. With two full slots, the new weapon replaces the active one and leaves the old weapon on the floor, allowing the player to reverse the choice.

Collision and lifecycle

Circle-against-rectangle collision keeps entities outside solid props. Enemy navigation combines clear-line pursuit, A* grid pathfinding, local corner steering, crowd separation, and stuck detection, so enemies route around furniture instead of repeatedly walking into it. Reverse iteration keeps removal, splitting, explosions, and area skills stable.

Originality

The requested reference was treated as a genre and control direction, not as a request to copy copyrighted assets or exact presentation. The result uses original geometric graphics and an academic research theme.

8. Challenges

Combat readability requires five loot colors, distinct enemy silhouettes, health bars, a boss bar, particles, and restrained screen shake. Balance values are centralized so fire rate, damage, magazines, health, speed, and drop rates can be tuned after playtesting.

9. Limitations

  • Balance is based on developer playtesting rather than a large player sample.
  • Best scores remain local to one browser.
  • Future work could add shops, weapon comparison panels, more bosses, controller support, and sound settings.

10. Reflection

The AI agent accelerated architecture, implementation, and verification, but human judgment remained necessary for scope, originality, visual style, balance, and honest documentation. The final application now includes room progression, loot generation, inventory decisions, reloading, collision-aware layouts, bosses, permanent upgrades, responsive controls, persistence, and repeatable tests.
